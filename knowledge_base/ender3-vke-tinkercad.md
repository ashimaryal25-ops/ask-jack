# Ender 3 V3 KE — Designing Your Own Model in Tinkercad
machine: Ender 3 V3 KE
category: 3D printing — custom design

## "I want to make something custom, not download an existing model"

If you can't find your object on a model site like Thingiverse, you can design your own. **Tinkercad** is a free, browser-based 3D design tool that's easy for complete beginners — no installation, no experience needed. When you're done, you export a **.stl** file and take it into the normal printing workflow.

---

## Step 1 — Open Tinkercad and sign in

1. Go to **www.tinkercad.com** in any web browser.
2. Create a free account (you can sign in with a Google or email account).
3. Click **Create** → **3D Design**. You'll see a flat blue grid called the **Workplane** — this is your build area.

---

## Step 2 — Add shapes

1. On the right side is a panel of **Basic Shapes** (box, cylinder, sphere, etc.).
2. Drag a shape onto the Workplane.
3. Click and drag the shape to move it. Use the small white handles on its corners to resize it, and the black handles to change its height.

Build your object by combining simple shapes — most objects are just boxes, cylinders, and spheres put together.

---

## Step 3 — Combine shapes

1. To join shapes into one object: select them (click and drag a box around them, or shift-click each), then click **Group** (top toolbar). They become a single solid object.

---

## Step 4 — Cut holes

1. Any shape can be turned into a **Hole** instead of solid — select it and click the **Hole** option in the shape settings (it turns striped/transparent).
2. Position the Hole shape where you want to cut, overlapping the solid part.
3. Select both the solid and the Hole, then click **Group**. The Hole carves itself out of the solid — this is how you make openings, slots, and hollow areas.

---

## Step 5 — Keep it within the printer's size

The Ender 3 V3 KE can print objects up to **220mm × 220mm × 240mm**. Tinkercad shows measurements in millimeters, so keep your design within those limits. If it needs to be bigger, you'd split it into parts or use the larger CR-M4 printer instead.

---

## Step 6 — Export your model

1. When your design is finished, click **Export** (top-right).
2. Choose **.STL**.
3. The .stl file downloads to your computer.

---

## Step 7 — Print it

You now have a .stl file, exactly like one downloaded from a model site. Take it into the normal process: open it in Cura, slice it, save the sliced file to a USB drive, and print. Ask me "how do I 3D print something?" and I'll walk you through slicing and printing from here.

---

## Tips for beginners

- Start simple. A nameplate, a keychain, or a simple box is a great first custom design.
- Flat-bottomed designs print best — they stick to the bed without needing supports.
- Tall, thin designs can tip over or need supports. Add a wide base if you can.
