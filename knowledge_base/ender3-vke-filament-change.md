# Ender 3 V3 KE — Changing Filament
machine: Ender 3 V3 KE
category: 3D printing — hardware / filament

## When do you need to change filament?
- The current spool color is wrong for your project
- The spool is empty or nearly empty
- You need a different material (e.g. switching from PLA to PETG)

## Safety first
- The nozzle heats to 200°C+ during this process — do not touch it
- Wear safety glasses

## Step-by-step: Changing filament

**1. Turn on the printer**
Flip the red power switch on the left side to ON.

**2. Wake the touchscreen**
Tap the screen.

**3. Open the Prepare menu**
Tap the **Prepare icon** (three lines with dots — looks like a settings/menu icon).

**4. Go to Extrude/Retract**
Tap the **"Extrude/Retract"** tab.

**5. Retract the old filament**
Tap the **Retract** button. The printer will:
- Heat the nozzle automatically
- Pull the filament back out of the extruder

**6. Pull out the old filament**
Once retracted, gently pull the filament out of the extruder tube. If it resists, tap Retract again.

**7. Wind up the old spool**
Wind the filament back onto the spool neatly. Tuck the loose end through one of the small holes on the edge of the spool so it does not unravel.

**8. Load the new filament**
- Cut the tip of the new filament at a **45° angle** and snap it off straight — this makes threading much easier
- Place the new spool on the filament holder on the back of the printer
- Thread the filament end through the **filament detector** (the small sensor on the frame)
- Press the extrusion clamp and push the filament through the hole all the way down to the heat break until you see a little bit poking out at the nozzle

**9. Extrude the new filament**
Tap the **Extrude** button. The printer will:
- Heat the nozzle
- Push the new filament through

Watch the nozzle — when the new filament color starts coming out consistently, the change is complete. You may see a mix of old and new colors briefly — this is normal.

**10. You are ready to print**
The printer is now loaded with the new filament.
