# Ender 3 V3 KE — Safety Rules
machine: Ender 3 V3 KE
category: 3D printing — safety

## Required PPE
- **Safety glasses** — must be worn while the printer is operating
- **Gloves** — recommended when handling materials, especially ABS

## Hazards to be aware of

**Burns**
The nozzle reaches 200°C or higher. The print bed also heats to 60°C+. Never touch either while the printer is running or cooling down.

**Fumes**
All filaments release some fumes when melted. PLA is generally safe. ABS releases styrene — a possible carcinogen — and requires good ventilation. Do not print ABS in an enclosed space without airflow.

**Electric shock**
Do not open the printer casing or touch internal electronics. Do not attempt repairs yourself — ask ICL staff.

**Moving parts**
Keep hands away from the print head, gantry, and bed while the printer is moving. Do not reach into the printer during a print. Do not manually push the nozzle or bed while the printer is powered on — the printer will auto-shut off as a protection measure.

**Do not wear loose gloves or accessories while the printer is moving** — they can get caught in moving parts. Gloves are fine when handling filament spools or removing cooled prints.

## Lab rules
- No eating or drinking in the lab
- Do not leave a print completely unattended during the first few minutes — confirm the first layer looks correct first
- Write your name and contact info on the dry erase board while your print is running
- Clean up after yourself — remove all scraps, prints, and material bits from the work area
- If anything looks wrong (smoke, burning smell, grinding noise) — power off immediately using the red switch and contact ICL staff

## Emergency
Power off using the red switch on the left side of the printer. ICL staff can be reached through the Gettysburg College ICL channels.
