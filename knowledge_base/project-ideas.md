# ICL Project Ideas — Impressive Things You Can Make

This document lists creative and impressive project ideas for each machine in the ICL. Use it to help students who ask "what can I make?" or "give me project ideas."

## 3D Printing Ideas (Ender 3 V3 KE / CR-M4)

**Flexi-articulated creature (octopus, dragon, axolotl, shark)**
Print a fully poseable creature in one piece — no assembly required. The segments are designed to flex right off the print bed. Extremely popular, always impresses people. Search "flexi dragon" or "flexi octopus" on Printables or Thingiverse. Print time: 2–4 hours in PLA.

**Planetary gear fidget cube**
All the gears mesh together and spin as a single unit. Prints in one piece with no supports or assembly. The mechanical complexity looks impossible from the outside but works perfectly. Search "planetary gear cube" on Printables. Print time: 3–5 hours.

**Cryptex combination lock**
A functional spinning combination lock — set the rings to the right letters and the cylinder opens. Great as a gift box, escape room prop, or just a desk showpiece. Search "cryptex" on Printables. Print time: 4–6 hours.

**Impossible ball-in-a-cage**
A ball trapped inside a lattice cage, printed as one piece. Looks like it physically shouldn't exist. A classic "how did they make that?" piece. Search "ball in cage" on Printables. Print time: 1–2 hours.

**Voronoi vase**
A vase made of connected polygon cells (Voronoi pattern) — structurally strong but looks impossibly delicate. Stunning with flowers or an LED inside. Search "voronoi vase" on Printables. Print in vase/spiralize mode for a seamless single-wall print. Print time: 2–4 hours.

**Custom cookie or clay stamp**
Design your name, initials, or a logo in any design tool, mirror it horizontally, and print it. Press it into cookie dough, polymer clay, or fondant. Great personalized gift. Easy to design, prints in under an hour.

**Personalized ring or bracelet**
Engrave your name or a pattern onto a wearable ring or cuff. Print in TPU for a flexible, comfortable wearable or PLA for a solid fashion piece. Search "name ring" or "name bracelet" on Printables. Print time: 30–60 minutes.

**Gyroscope / gimbal**
A nested ring gimbal that keeps its center level no matter how you tilt it. Classic physics demo, surprisingly meditative to hold and spin. Search "3D printed gyroscope" on Printables. Print time: 2–3 hours.

**Miniature chess set**
Print your own chess pieces — custom designs, tiny scale. Pair with a laser-engraved board for a full custom set. Many free sets on Printables. Print time: 1–2 hours per set of pieces.

**Articulated hand / skeleton hand**
A life-size hand with working finger joints, printed in one piece. Pull the tendons (strings) to curl the fingers. Very dramatic and crowd-pleasing. Search "articulated hand" on Printables. Print time: 4–6 hours.

## Laser Cutting Ideas

**Interlocking puzzle box**
A wooden box that only opens if you push, slide, or rotate the panels in the right sequence. No glue or hardware — just friction-fit laser-cut wood. Perfect gift box for hiding something inside. Intermediate skill level.

**Layered topographic map**
Stack laser-cut wood or acrylic layers to build a 3D contour map of your campus, hometown, or any terrain (export from terrain2stl.com). Mount it framed on the wall. Always gets compliments.

**Edge-lit acrylic nightlight**
Engrave a design — college crest, skyline, name, or mandala — into acrylic sheet. Edge-light it with LEDs. The engraved lines glow brilliantly while the rest of the sheet stays clear. Combine with a 3D-printed or laser-cut base housing the LED strip.

**Custom engraved wood coasters or cutting board**
Laser-engrave a photo, logo, or pattern into wood. One of the most giftable things in the lab — fast to make, looks professional.

**Jigsaw puzzle from a photo**
Upload a photo, generate a jigsaw cut pattern, and laser-cut it into wood or thick acrylic. A completely custom puzzle. Great gift for a team photo, campus landmark, or pet portrait.

**Mechanical wooden calendar**
Rotating wooden discs or sliding tabs show day, month, and date. No electronics — purely mechanical. Elegant desk accessory. Many free files available online for laser cutters.

**Honeycomb wall shelf set**
Interlocking hexagonal shelf modules that slot together and can be rearranged in any pattern. Mount on the wall for a modular display shelf.

## Vinyl Cutter Ideas

**Multi-layer laptop or water bottle decal**
Cut vinyl in multiple colors to build up a layered, detailed design — logo, illustration, portrait. Layer the colors one at a time for a professional look. Fast and cheap to make.

**Custom iron-on T-shirt design**
Use heat transfer vinyl (HTV) to put any design on a shirt, bag, or hoodie. Cut it on the vinyl cutter, press it with a heat press. Great for club merch or personalized gifts.

**Custom sticker sheet**
Cut a full sheet of small stickers — your drawings, icons, name designs. Print on sticker paper and cut the outlines with the vinyl cutter (print-then-cut workflow).

## Poster / Plotter Ideas

**Stippled portrait**
Convert a photo to a dense stipple dot pattern and let the plotter draw it with a fine-tip pen. Your face, a friend's face, or a pet — rendered in thousands of tiny dots. Looks like fine art. Software: Stipple.js or Inkscape with a stipple extension.

**Generative algorithmic art**
Mathematical patterns like Hilbert space-filling curves, rose curves, Lissajous figures, or Voronoi diagrams drawn in pen at poster size. Looks like something from a gallery. Generate the paths in Processing, Python, or p5.js.

**Hand-drawn campus map**
A precise pen-and-ink style map of Gettysburg College campus or your hometown, plotted at large format. Great for a dorm room print.

**Waveform art print**
Plot the audio waveform of a meaningful song or a recording of your own voice. Frame it. Looks like abstract art but has personal meaning. Generate the SVG path from any audio file using free tools online.

**TicTacToe / game board**
Draw a game board and have the plotter "play" by marking positions during the game. An interactive demo that always surprises people — the plotter draws in real time as moves are made.

## Electronics / Arduino Ideas

**Theremin**
A touchless musical instrument: move your hand near an ultrasonic sensor and the Arduino produces a musical tone through a buzzer or speaker. The pitch changes with distance. Zero assembly experience needed — works on a breadboard. Sci-fi vibes, very crowd-pleasing.

**RFID mystery box**
A physical box wired to a solenoid latch. Only unlocks when you tap it with the right RFID card or fob. Great escape room prop, gift box, or tech demo. Combines electronics with a 3D-printed or laser-cut enclosure.

**LED matrix scrolling name badge**
A wearable badge with an 8×8 LED matrix that scrolls your name, a message, or an animation. Wearable, programmable, and very visible at events. Works with an Arduino Nano and the MAX7219 chip.

**Reaction time tester**
An LED lights up at a random interval. Press a button as fast as you can. The display shows your reaction time in milliseconds. Simple build, but irresistible — people will compete for the fastest time. Great party prop.

**Plant moisture sensor**
A probe that measures soil moisture. When the plant is too dry, an LED turns red and a buzzer sounds. A genuinely useful gadget that also teaches analog sensor reading and thresholds. Build time: 1 hour.

**Simon Says memory game**
Four colored LEDs and four buttons. The Arduino plays a pattern; you repeat it. Classic, fully buildable in one session. Great first electronics project that actually becomes a game you can play.

**Mini weather station**
A DHT22 temperature and humidity sensor wired to a small OLED display. Shows live temperature, humidity, and a simple comfort index. 3D-print or laser-cut a small enclosure. Genuinely useful.

**Digital sound-reactive LED strip**
A microphone module feeds into an Arduino which drives a strip of WS2812 LEDs. The lights react to sound — louder = more lights, beat = pulse. Great dorm room accent light.

## Multi-Machine Combo Projects

**Custom glowing nightlight**
3D-print the housing, laser-cut the front acrylic diffuser panel with a custom engraved design, add a USB LED strip inside. Fully personalized, looks like a retail product. Combines the 3D printer, laser cutter, and basic electronics.

**Personalized chess set**
3D-print custom chess pieces (your own designs or downloaded), laser-engrave a wooden board with the grid and coordinate letters. The complete set is 100% custom-made in the ICL.

**RFID escape room prop box**
Laser-cut the wooden box, 3D-print the decorative elements, wire an Arduino + RFID reader to a solenoid latch inside. The box only opens when the right card is tapped. Full multi-machine build with a satisfying result.

**Custom music box**
Laser-cut the wooden housing and frame, 3D-print the pin roller (with your melody encoded as bumps at specific positions), and add a steel comb mechanism. Programs any simple melody into a physical rotating object.
