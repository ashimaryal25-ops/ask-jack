# Ender 3 V3 KE — Printing (Hardware Steps)
machine: Ender 3 V3 KE
category: 3D printing — hardware / printing

[VIDEO: https://xyaewwhcelutcoosbawk.supabase.co/storage/v1/object/public/videos/Printing.mp4 | Full walkthrough: USB to printer — inserting your file and starting a print]

## Before you start
- Make sure you have a .gcode file saved on a USB drive
- Put on safety glasses
- Check that the print bed is clean and clear of old prints or debris

## Step-by-step: Starting a print

**1. Turn on the printer**
Flip the red power switch on the left side of the printer to ON.

**2. Wake the touchscreen**
Tap the touchscreen to wake it from sleep.

**3. Insert the USB drive**
Insert your USB drive into either USB port on the printer.

⚠️ **Important USB rules:**
- Your .gcode file must be in the **root directory** of the USB — not inside any folder
- Name your file using only letters, numbers, and common characters (no special symbols)
- Do NOT remove the USB drive while the print is running

**4. Navigate to your file**
- Tap the **File icon** at the bottom of the touchscreen
- Select the **"USB Drive"** tab
- Find and tap your .gcode file

**5. Review and confirm**
A details screen will show your file name, estimated time, and material usage. Confirm this looks correct.

**6. Tap "Print"**
The printer will automatically:
- Level the bed (takes about 1–2 minutes)
- Heat the nozzle to print temperature
- Heat the bed
- Begin printing

**7. Watch the first layer**
Stay and watch the first 2–3 minutes. The first layer is the most critical.
- Good first layer: plastic sticks flat, lines are smooth and slightly squished
- Bad first layer: plastic curls up, doesn't stick, or looks like spaghetti

If the first layer looks wrong, cancel the print and ask ICL staff for help rather than wasting material.

**8. Write your name on the dry erase board**
Write your name and contact info on the dry erase board near the printer so staff and other students know the printer is in use.

**9. Come back when it's done**
You do not need to stay. The printer stops automatically when finished.

## Removing your print
1. Wait for the bed to cool down (the screen shows bed temperature)
2. The bed is magnetic and flexible — **lift the entire bed plate off**
3. Flex the bed gently — the print will pop off cleanly
4. Replace the bed plate flat on the printer before leaving

[IMAGE: https://xyaewwhcelutcoosbawk.supabase.co/storage/v1/object/public/Photos/Scraper.jpg | The scraper tool — located next to the monitor. Use this to remove stubborn prints from the bed.]

## After printing
- Remove all scraps and leftover filament from the bed and surrounding area
- Erase your name from the dry erase board
- Leave the printer ready for the next user

## If something goes wrong
- **Print not sticking to bed**: Apply a thin layer of glue stick evenly on the bed surface, then try again. This is the most common fix.
- **Residual filament stuck to bed**: Let bed cool, scrape gently with a blade, or flex the magnetic plate to pop it off.
- **Spaghetti print (filament going everywhere)**: Cancel print, clean bed, check that supports are enabled in Cura if needed.
- **Filament not coming out**: Nozzle may be clogged — ask ICL staff.
- **Printer makes grinding noise**: Stop print immediately, ask ICL staff.
- **Print stopped mid-way**: The printer has power loss recovery — if power cut, it can resume. For other stops check the filament detection sensor (it pauses if filament runs out).
