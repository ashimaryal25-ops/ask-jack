# Ender 3 V3 KE — Finding or Designing a 3D Model
machine: Ender 3 V3 KE
category: 3D printing — model preparation

## What file format does the printer need?
The printer needs a **.gcode** file. You do not create this directly — you start with a **.stl** or **.obj** file and convert it using slicing software (Ultimaker Cura).

## Option A — Download a model someone already made (easiest for beginners)
You do not need to design anything. Thousands of free models are available online.

**Recommended sites:**
- **Thingiverse** (www.thingiverse.com) — largest free library, search for almost anything
- **Printables** (www.printables.com) — high quality community models
- **CGTrader** (www.cgtrader.com) — mix of free and paid
- **Sketchfab** (www.sketchfab.com) — good for organic and artistic models

**How to download:**
1. Go to one of the sites above
2. Search for what you want (e.g. "dog figurine", "phone stand", "name tag")
3. Look for files tagged as STL or OBJ
4. Click Download — choose the .stl file if multiple formats are offered
5. Save the file to a USB drive or your computer

## Option B — Design your own model (for custom objects)
Use **Tinkercad** (www.tinkercad.com) — free, browser-based, no installation needed. Best for beginners making boxes, name tags, brackets, or anything geometric.

For more advanced design:
- Autodesk Fusion (free for students)
- Blender (free, steep learning curve, best for organic shapes)

**Export your design as .stl or .obj before moving to the next step.**

## Tips for picking a good model
- Check that the model dimensions will fit within 8.66 × 8.66 × 9.4 inches
- Look at the "likes" or "makes" count — popular models print more reliably
- Read comments on the model page — other users often mention print settings or issues
- Avoid models with very thin walls or floating parts (hard to print without supports)
