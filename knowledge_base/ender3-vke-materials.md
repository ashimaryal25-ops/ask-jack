# Ender 3 V3 KE — Materials
machine: Ender 3 V3 KE
category: 3D printing — materials

## What material should I use?
Use **PLA** for almost every beginner project. It is the easiest to print, safe, and works well for decorative objects, prototypes, stands, signs, and models.

## PLA (Polylactic Acid) — Recommended for beginners
- Most common material at the ICL
- Safe, low odor, made from plant starch
- Print temperature: around 200°C nozzle, 60°C bed (Cura sets this automatically)
- Best for: figurines, stands, signs, prototypes, event props, decorative objects
- Store away from moisture and heat (below 50°C)
- Color options: varies by what spools the ICL currently has stocked

## PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol)
- Stronger and more heat-resistant than PLA
- Slightly harder to print — not recommended for first-time users
- Print temperature: around 230°C nozzle, 70°C bed
- Best for: parts that need to handle heat, stress, or outdoor use
- Must be kept dry — store with silica gel to prevent moisture absorption

## ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene)
- Hard, impact-resistant plastic
- Releases styrene fumes when printing — requires good ventilation
- Not recommended for beginners
- Best for: mechanical parts requiring high durability

## Safety with all filaments
- Wear safety glasses when the printer is running
- Do not touch the nozzle — it reaches 200°C or higher
- Wash hands after handling filament
- Do not eat or drink in the lab
- PLA and PETG are safe for general use; ABS requires ventilation

## Where are the filament spools?
Ask ICL staff or check the labeled storage area in Plank 117. Filament spools are loaded onto the holder on the back-right of the Ender 3 V3 KE.
