# Ender 3 V3 KE — Overview
machine: Ender 3 V3 KE
category: 3D printing
location: Design Studio, Plank 117, Gettysburg College ICL

## What is this machine?
The Creality Ender 3 V3 KE is a desktop FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) 3D printer. It melts plastic filament and builds objects layer by layer. It is the primary 3D printer available to students at the ICL.

## What can it make?
Almost any solid plastic object: phone stands, name tags, replacement parts, models, prototypes, decorative objects, enclosures, brackets, keychains, event props, and more.

## Build volume
220mm × 220mm × 240mm (8.66" × 8.66" × 9.45"). Objects larger than this must be split into parts and assembled.

## Key specs
- Nozzle diameter: 0.4mm (standard)
- Layer thickness: 0.1–0.35mm
- Typical print speed: 300mm/s (max 500mm/s)
- Precision: ±0.1mm
- Bed leveling: Automatic (CR-Touch sensor)
- Filament detection sensor: Yes (pauses print if filament runs out)
- Power loss recovery: Yes (resumes print after power outage)
- Rated power: 350W

## Materials it uses
PLA, PETG, ABS, TPU (95A), ASA filament. PLA is the default and recommended material for most beginner projects.

## How long does a print take?
Small objects (keychains, small figurines): 30 minutes to 2 hours.
Medium objects (phone stand, small box): 2–5 hours.
Large objects: 5–12+ hours. Plan ahead, especially if you have a deadline.

## Where is it?
Design Studio, Plank 117, Gettysburg College. The ICL is open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week to all students, faculty, and staff.

## Large format alternative
For objects larger than 8.66 × 8.66 × 9.4 inches, use the Creality CR-M4 (build volume 17.7″ × 17.7″ × 18.5″), also located in Plank 117.
